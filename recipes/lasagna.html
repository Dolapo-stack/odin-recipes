Lasagna

Description

Lasagna is a classic Italian dish made of layers of wide, flat pasta sheets alternated with rich fillings such as seasoned ground meat (often beef or pork), tomato sauce, béchamel or ricotta cheese, and melted mozzarella. Each layer is carefully stacked and baked to perfection, creating a hearty, flavorful casserole with a golden, bubbling top.

Lasagna is known for its comforting, savory taste and is often served as a main course at gatherings or special meals. Variations can include vegetables, chicken, seafood, or be made entirely vegetarian.

Ingredients

For the Meat Sauce:

  • 500g ground beef (or half beef, half pork)
  • 1 small onion, chopped
  • 2–3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 400g (1 can) crushed tomatoes
  • 2 tablespoons tomato paste
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon dried basil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Olive oil (for sautéing)

For the Cheese Layer:

  • 250g ricotta cheese (or cottage cheese)
  • 1 egg (optional, for binding)
  • 1 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 2 cups shredded mozzarella cheese

For the Pasta:

  • 9–12 lasagna sheets (no-boil or regular, depending on type)

    • Steps

    1. Prepare the Meat Sauce:
      • Heat olive oil in a pan.
      • Add chopped onions and cook until translucent.
      • Add minced garlic and stir briefly.
      • Add ground meat and cook until browned.
      • Stir in tomato paste, tomato sauce/crushed tomatoes.
      • Season with salt, pepper, and Italian herbs.
      • Simmer for 15–20 minutes until thick and flavorful.
    2. Prepare the Ricotta Mixture:
      • In a bowl, mix ricotta cheese with a beaten egg, salt, pepper, and some grated Parmesan. (Optional: Add chopped parsley.)
    3. Boil Lasagna Sheets (if not using no-boil type):
      • Cook according to package instructions. Drain and lay flat on a clean surface.
