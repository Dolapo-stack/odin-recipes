Moo Shu Pork

Description

Moo Shu Pork is a popular Chinese-American stir-fry dish

These ingredients are cooked together in a savory-sweet hoisin and soy sauce blend and traditionally served with thin Mandarin pancakes or flour tortillas and additional hoisin sauce for wrapping. It’s flavorful, tender, and offers a mix of textures—crunchy, soft, and juicy.

Ingredients

  • Shredded pork (often marinated for flavor)
  • Scrambled eggs
  • Wood ear mushrooms or shiitake mushrooms
  • Cabbage, carrots, and scallions

Steps

  1. Prepare Ingredients:
    • Slice pork (tenderloin or shoulder) thinly.
    • Marinate pork with soy sauce, cornstarch, and a little sesame oil (10–15 mins).
    • Beat 2 eggs and season lightly with salt.
    • Julienne or shred: cabbage, carrots, wood ear mushrooms (rehydrated if dried), and scallions.
    • Mince garlic and ginger.
  2. Cook the Eggs:
    • Heat a little oil in a wok or large pan.
    • Scramble the eggs lightly and remove from pan once cooked.
